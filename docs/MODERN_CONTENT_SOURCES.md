## 1️⃣ TED Talks - Inspirational & Educational Talks

### **Legal Status: ✅ FREE TO USE**

**License:** [Creative Commons BY-NC-ND 4.0](https://www.ted.com/about/our-organization/our-policies-terms/ted-talks-usage-policy)

**What You Can Do:**
- ✅ Use transcripts for non-commercial educational purposes
- ✅ Translate, simplify, and adapt text
- ✅ Display with proper attribution
- ✅ Use for freemium model (free tier exists)

**What You Cannot Do:**
- ❌ Edit video content itself
- ❌ Use solely for direct commercial gain (but freemium is OK)
- ❌ Claim ownership or remove attribution

### **Content Characteristics:**
- **Length:** 5-18 minutes (1,500-3,500 words)
- **Emotional Impact:** Very High (speakers trained to create emotional moments)
- **Topics:** Psychology, inspiration, science, technology, relationships, health
- **Language:** Contemporary, conversational, accessible
- **Validation:** Millions of views = proven engagement

### **How to Access:**
- **Transcripts:** Available on TED.com for every talk (click "Transcript" tab)
- **API:** TED has no official API, but transcripts are scrapable (with attribution)
- **Languages:** Pre-translated in 100+ languages (but we'll simplify English)

### **Selection Criteria for BookBridge:**
1. **Emotional resonance** - Stories that make people cry, laugh, or feel inspired
2. **Universal themes** - Love, loss, identity, belonging, overcoming obstacles
3. **Clear narrative arc** - Beginning, middle, end (not just data/facts)
4. **ESL-friendly vocabulary** - Avoid heavy jargon, technical terms
5. **Length** - 2,000-3,000 words ideal (10-15 min talks)
6. **View count** - 1M+ views preferred (proven engagement)

## 2️⃣ StoryCorps - Intimate Conversation Archive

### **Legal Status: ✅ FREE TO USE (Public Domain)**

**License:** Public domain / Creative Commons (archived by Library of Congress)

**What You Can Do:**
- ✅ Use transcripts freely
- ✅ Adapt, simplify, translate
- ✅ No permission needed (public archive)

### **Content Characteristics:**
- **Length:** 2-10 minutes (400-2,000 words)
- **Emotional Impact:** Very High (real people, real stories)
- **Topics:** Family, love, loss, memories, life lessons, relationships
- **Language:** Conversational, authentic, deeply personal
- **Validation:** NPR partnership, Peabody Award winner

### **What Makes StoryCorps Special:**
- Real conversations between loved ones (mother-daughter, friends, couples)
- Extremely intimate and emotional
- Short, digestible format
- Universal themes that transcend culture
- Many feature immigrants/ESL speakers (relatable!)

### **How to Access:**
- **Website:** https://storycorps.org/stories/
- **Archive:** 60,000+ stories in Library of Congress
- **Transcripts:** Many have transcripts available on site
- **Search:** Filter by theme, relationship, location

### **Selection Criteria for BookBridge:**
1. **Transcripts available** - Not all stories have transcripts
2. **Clear audio** - For potential audio narration
3. **Universal themes** - Immigration, family, overcoming obstacles
4. **Emotional arc** - Stories with clear beginning/middle/end
5. **ESL-friendly** - Simple language, not heavy dialect
6. **Length** - 500-1,500 words ideal

### **Top StoryCorps Themes to Explore:**
- **Immigration stories** - "Coming to America" collection (highly relatable for ESL)
- **Love stories** - "Great Love" collection (universal emotion)
- **Overcoming adversity** - "Finding Our Way" collection
- **Family bonds** - "Family" collection (parent-child conversations)
- **Life lessons** - "Wisdom" collection (elderly sharing insights)

### **Sample Stories to Review:**

2. **"Danny & Annie"** - Love story (married 60+ years)
3. **"Mama's Boots"** - Immigration/sacrifice (powerful)

### **Contact Information:**

## 3️⃣ The Moth - True Personal Stories Told Live

### **Legal Status: ⚠️ REQUIRES PERMISSION**

**License:** Stories are copyrighted by individual storytellers, but The Moth often grants educational use

**What You Can Do (with permission):**
- ✅ Use transcripts for educational purposes (likely)
- ✅ Adapt for ESL learning (with attribution)

**What You Cannot Do (without permission):**
- ❌ Use stories without contacting The Moth
- ❌ Commercial use without licensing

### **Content Characteristics:**
- **Length:** 5-15 minutes (1,200-3,000 words)
- **Emotional Impact:** Very High ("made me cry" factor)
- **Topics:** Personal transformation, overcoming obstacles, identity, relationships
- **Language:** Storytelling format, narrative arc, accessible
- **Validation:** 25+ years, Peabody Award, millions of podcast listeners

### **What Makes The Moth Special:**
- Stories told without notes (authentic, conversational)
- 5-minute rule: Must have clear beginning, middle, end
- Stakes: All stories involve personal risk/transformation
- Extremely curated (only best stories make it to stage)
- Proven completion rate (podcast listeners finish episodes)

### **How to Access:**
- **Website:** https://themoth.org/stories
- **Podcast:** The Moth Radio Hour (transcripts available on request)
- **Archive:** 1,000+ stories searchable by theme

### **Selection Criteria for BookBridge:**
1. **Universal themes** - Not location-specific or culturally niche
2. **Clear narrative arc** - Beginning (setup), middle (conflict), end (resolution)
3. **Emotional payoff** - Makes you laugh, cry, or feel inspired
4. **ESL-friendly** - Conversational language, not heavy slang
5. **Length** - 5-10 min stories (1,500-2,500 words)
6. **Transcript availability** - Some stories have transcripts

### **Top Moth Themes to Explore:**
- **Coming of Age** - Identity, growing up, self-discovery
- **Love Hurts** - Relationships, heartbreak, reconciliation
- **Fish Out of Water** - Immigration, cultural adjustment (perfect for ESL!)
- **Triumph** - Overcoming obstacles, personal victories

### **Sample Stories to Review (Request Transcripts):**

4. **"My Father's Boots"** by Ophira Eisenberg - Father-daughter (moving)
5. **"The Bridge"** by Malcolm Gladwell - Redemption story

### **Contact Information:**

- **Educational Use Inquiry:** info@themoth.org
