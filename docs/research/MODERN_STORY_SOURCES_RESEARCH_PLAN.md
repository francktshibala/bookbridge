# Modern Story Sources Research Plan

## 📚 Overview

**Goal**: Discover and validate 50-100 modern story sources that meet BookBridge's emotional impact criteria, ensuring legal compliance and ESL learner resonance. This research will identify concrete sources, specific stories, and create an actionable implementation roadmap.

**Timeline**: 5-day research sprint  
**Expected Outcome**: Comprehensive source catalog with 50+ validated stories ready for extraction and implementation

**Strategic Context**: 
- BookBridge needs modern, emotionally impactful stories to become a must-have ESL app
- Stories must be story-driven (not fact-driven), 15-45 minutes long, with clear emotional arcs
- Approach: Extract themes from multiple sources, rewrite as original narratives (no copyright issues)
- Target: 50-100 stories across diverse themes (immigration, education, transformation, family, health, community)

---

## 🎯 Research Objectives

### Primary Goals
1. **Source Discovery**: Identify 20+ reliable sources for modern stories (long-form journalism, documentaries, interviews, memoirs)
2. **Story Validation**: Validate 50+ specific stories against Step 0.25 and Step 0.5 criteria (story-driven, emotional arc, ESL resonance)
3. **Legal Safety**: Ensure all sources and extraction methods are legally sound (no copyright violations)
4. **Implementation Roadmap**: Create prioritized list with extraction strategies and implementation timeline

### Success Criteria
- ✅ 20+ diverse sources identified with access methods documented
- ✅ 50+ stories validated against emotional impact criteria
- ✅ 100% legal compliance confirmed for extraction approach
- ✅ Prioritized implementation plan with 5 pilot stories ready
- ✅ Clear extraction workflow documented for each source type

## 👥 Agent Research Division

### Agent 1: Source Discovery & Content Curation Expert
**Focus**: Discovering exceptional sources for modern stories  
**Deliverable**: `docs/research/Agent1_Source_Discovery_Findings.md`

**Your Mission**: Think like a content curator discovering hidden gems. Your job is to find sources that contain stories so powerful, so emotionally engaging, that ESL learners will text their friends about them. Don't limit yourself to obvious sources—explore creatively and discover sources we haven't considered.

**What Makes a Great Source**:
- **Story-driven content** (first-person narratives, emotional journeys) NOT fact-driven (biographies, encyclopedias)
- **Modern relevance** (2020s preferred, 2010s acceptable) - stories that feel current
- **Accessible** (can we actually get the content? API, download, manual access)
- **Rich emotional moments** (struggles, breakthroughs, transformations)
- **ESL resonance potential** (themes like immigration, education, overcoming barriers)

**Research Approach**:
1. **Creative Exploration** (4 hours)
   - Explore ANY source type that might contain story-driven content
   - Think beyond journalism: documentaries, podcasts, interviews, memoirs, speeches, oral histories, user-generated content, community archives
   - Discover sources we haven't thought of - be creative and exploratory
   - Document access methods (API, manual download, web scraping, etc.)
   - Focus on finding sources with emotional depth, not just information

2. **Source Evaluation** (3 hours)
   - Assess each discovered source: Does it contain story-driven content?
   - Evaluate length potential: Can stories reach 15-45 minutes when fully developed?
   - Assess modern relevance: Are stories from 2020s or 2010s?
   - Evaluate theme diversity: What themes are represented?
   - Create prioritization matrix: Which sources yield highest-quality stories?

3. **Story Discovery** (3 hours)
   - Identify 50+ specific stories from discovered sources
   - Document story titles, sources, and access methods
   - Preliminary emotional impact assessment: Does it have a clear emotional arc?
   - ESL resonance multiplier identification: Which multipliers apply?

**Deliverables**:
- Comprehensive source catalog with access methods
- 50+ story candidates with source links and preliminary validation
- Source prioritization matrix (ease of access × story quality × modern relevance)
- Recommended starting sources for pilot phase

### Agent 2: Legal & Copyright Compliance Specialist
**Focus**: Ensuring legal safety while enabling creative discovery  
**Deliverable**: `docs/research/Agent2_Legal_Compliance_Findings.md`

**Your Mission**: Be the legal guardian who enables creative exploration, not limits it. Your job is to ensure we can safely extract stories from ANY source Agent 1 discovers, while maintaining legal compliance. Think creatively about licensing options and fair use arguments.

**Core Principle**: We extract themes and emotional moments (not text), then rewrite as original narratives. This is legally sound—but we need to verify it works for each source type discovered.

**Research Approach**:
1. **Copyright Analysis** (3 hours)
   - Review extraction approach: Extract themes/emotional moments, not text
   - Analyze fair use and transformative use arguments for our approach
   - Identify copyright boundaries: What's protected (specific expression) vs. not protected (facts, ideas, themes)
   - Review similar approaches: How do biopics, documentaries, historical fiction handle this?
   - Document legal foundation: Why is our approach legally sound?

2. **Source Licensing Research** (3 hours)
   - For EACH source Agent 1 discovers, analyze licensing terms
   - Identify Creative Commons sources (what are the terms?)
   - Identify public domain sources (government archives, historical content)
   - Identify sources requiring attribution
   - Identify sources with fair use potential
   - Document licensing status for ALL discovered sources (not just obvious ones)

3. **Risk Assessment** (2 hours)
   - Identify potential legal risks in extraction process
   - Document mitigation strategies for each risk
   - Create "safe extraction" guidelines that work for ANY source type
   - Recommend legal review triggers: When should we consult a lawyer?

**Deliverables**:
- Legal safety assessment of extraction approach
- Source-by-source licensing analysis
- Risk mitigation strategies
- Safe extraction workflow guidelines
- Legal review checklist for new sources
