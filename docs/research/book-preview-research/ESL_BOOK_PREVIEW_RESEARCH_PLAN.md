# ESL Book Preview Research Plan

**Research Goal:** Determine optimal preview/overview content and format to maximize ESL student engagement, reduce anxiety, and increase completion rates.

**Research Question:** What preview content, format, and timing best help ESL students decide to read a book and successfully complete it?

---

## Overview

### Problem Statement

Currently, BookBridge books start immediately with the first sentence. However, platforms like Audible provide previews/overviews that:
- Set expectations about content, difficulty, and length
- Provide context and background information
- Build anticipation and engagement
- Reduce anxiety and fear of failure

**Research Question:** Should BookBridge add previews? If yes, what content, format, and timing optimize ESL student success?

### Goals

1. **Understand Preview Impact**
   - How do previews affect completion rates?
   - Do previews reduce abandonment in first few sentences?
   - Do previews increase confidence and engagement?

2. **Identify Optimal Preview Content**
   - What information is most valuable? (plot summary, difficulty, length, themes)
   - What information is too much? (spoilers, overwhelming context)
   - What creates "I must read this" motivation?

3. **Determine Best Format**
   - Text-only previews vs. audio previews vs. mixed media
   - Length: short (30 seconds) vs. medium (2 minutes) vs. long (5 minutes)
   - Visual elements: images, illustrations, character introductions

4. **Establish Timing & Placement**
   - When should preview appear? (before book selection, after selection, both)
   - Should preview be skippable or required?
   - Should preview be re-accessible during reading?

5. **Create Preview Template**
   - Standardized preview structure for all books
   - Guidelines for generating previews for new books
   - Quality standards and validation criteria

## Research Methodology

### Multi-Agent Expert Analysis

Following the successful ESL book curation research methodology, we'll use 4 expert agents:

1. **Agent 1: ESL Reading Specialist**
   - Focus: What preview content helps ESL students most?
   - Expertise: 20+ years teaching ESL reading, knows student pain points

2. **Agent 2: UX/Product Researcher**
   - Focus: How do successful platforms handle previews?
   - Expertise: Audible, Kindle, language learning apps, best practices

3. **Agent 3: Cognitive Load Researcher**
   - Focus: How much context helps vs. overwhelms?
   - Expertise: Information processing, cognitive load theory, ESL comprehension

4. **Agent 4: Student Psychology Expert**
   - Focus: What preview elements reduce anxiety and build confidence?
   - Expertise: Motivation, anxiety reduction, confidence building

5. **Synthesis Agent**
   - Combines all findings into actionable preview template and guidelines

## Context & Constraints

### Current BookBridge Context

- **Current State:** Books start immediately with first sentence
- **Target Audience:** University ESL students (A2-B2 levels primarily)
- **Platform Features:** Synchronized audio, word-level highlighting, CEFR-level simplifications
- **Current Books:** 10 books (The Necklace, The Dead, The Metamorphosis, etc.)
- **Goal:** Expand to 75+ books with previews that maximize engagement

### Constraints

- **Preview Length:** Must be concise (students want to start reading)
- **Language Level:** Previews must be accessible to A2-B2 students
- **Format:** Must work with existing BookBridge UI/UX
- **Generation:** Must be scalable (can generate previews for 75+ books)
- **Spoilers:** Must avoid major plot spoilers
- **Cultural Sensitivity:** Must work across diverse student backgrounds
