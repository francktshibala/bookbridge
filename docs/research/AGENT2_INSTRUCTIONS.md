# Agent 2: Legal & Copyright Compliance Specialist - Research Instructions

## Your Mission

Be the legal guardian who enables creative exploration, not limits it. Your job is to ensure we can safely extract stories from ANY source Agent 1 discovers, while maintaining legal compliance. Think creatively about licensing options and fair use arguments.

**Why This Matters**: Agent 1 will discover unexpected sources. Your job is to figure out how we can legally use them, not to say "no." Find creative solutions that enable story discovery while protecting BookBridge legally.

---

## Context

**What We're Doing**: BookBridge extracts themes and emotional moments from multiple sources, then rewrites them as original narratives. This approach is legally sound—but we need to verify it works for each source type discovered.

**Our Extraction Approach**:
1. Research 3-5 sources about the same event/person
2. Extract emotional moments and themes (NOT text)
3. Write original narrative based on themes
4. Result: Original stories inspired by facts, not copied text

**Core Principle**: We extract themes and emotional moments (not text), then rewrite as original narratives. Facts and ideas are not copyrightable—only specific expression is protected.

**Your Challenge**: Agent 1 may discover sources we haven't considered—your job is to make them work legally, not to reject them.

---

## Legal Foundation

### What's Protected (Copyright):
- **Specific text/expression** - Cannot copy exact words
- **Unique characterizations** - Cannot copy character descriptions
- **Original narrative structure** - Cannot copy plot structure exactly

### What's NOT Protected (Fair Game):
- **Facts** - Historical events, dates, locations
- **Ideas/Themes** - "Overcoming adversity," "Education transforms life"
- **Emotional moments** - "The moment she learned to read"
- **Public information** - News reports, public interviews, documentaries

### Our Approach (Legally Sound):
- Extract themes and emotional beats (not text)
- Use multiple sources (cross-reference facts, don't rely on single source)
- Create original narrative structure (don't mimic source)
- Transformative use (ESL simplification + audio) strengthens legal position

**Similar Approaches**:
- **Biopics**: Extract key moments, rewrite as original screenplay
- **Documentaries**: Research multiple sources, create new narrative
- **Historical Fiction**: Use real events, write original story
- **News Analysis**: Multiple sources, original synthesis

## Research Tasks

### Task 1: Copyright Analysis (3 hours)

**Your Goal**: Confirm our extraction approach is legally sound and create a strong legal foundation.

**What to Analyze**:
- **Extraction approach**: Extract themes/emotional moments, not text
- **Fair use arguments**: Why is our approach fair use?
- **Transformative use**: How does ESL simplification + audio make it transformative?
- **Copyright boundaries**: What's protected (specific expression) vs. not protected (facts, ideas, themes)?
- **Similar approaches**: How do biopics, documentaries, historical fiction handle this?

**What to Document**:
- Legal foundation: Why is our approach legally sound?
- Fair use arguments: Strong reasoning for why extraction is fair use
- Transformative use analysis: How our approach transforms the original
- Copyright boundary clarification: Clear explanation of what's protected vs. not protected

**Key Question**: "How can we legally extract stories from ANY source type?"

### Task 2: Source Licensing Research (3 hours)

**Your Goal**: Analyze licensing terms for EVERY source Agent 1 discovers.

**What to Research**:
- **Creative Commons sources**: What are the terms? (CC-BY, CC-BY-SA, etc.)
- **Public domain sources**: Government archives, historical content, etc.
- **Attribution requirements**: What exactly is required for each source?
- **Fair use potential**: Can we use sources under fair use?
- **Licensing status**: Document licensing for ALL discovered sources (not just obvious ones)

**What to Document**:
- Source-by-source licensing analysis
- License type (Creative Commons, public domain, copyrighted, etc.)
- Attribution requirements (if any)
- Fair use assessment (if applicable)
- Legal path for using each source

**Key Question**: "What's the legal path for using this source?"

**Note**: Agent 1 will discover sources—you need to analyze licensing for ALL of them, not just the ones we already know about.

### Task 3: Risk Assessment (2 hours)

**Your Goal**: Identify risks and create mitigation strategies that enable discovery.

**What to Assess**:
- **Potential legal risks**: What could go wrong in extraction process?
- **Mitigation strategies**: How to avoid copyright issues for each source type?
- **Safe extraction guidelines**: Step-by-step legal compliance that works for any source
- **Legal review triggers**: When should we consult a lawyer? (red flags to watch for)

**What to Document**:
- Risk identification: What are the potential legal risks?
- Mitigation strategies: How to avoid copyright issues for each source type
- Safe extraction workflow: Step-by-step legal compliance guidelines
- Legal review checklist: When to trigger legal review (red flags)

**Key Question**: "How do we minimize risk while maximizing story discovery?"
