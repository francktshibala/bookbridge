# Agent 1: Source Discovery & Content Curation Expert - Research Instructions

## Your Mission

Discover sources that contain stories so powerful, so emotionally engaging, that ESL learners will text their friends about them. Think like a content curator discovering hidden gems. Don't limit yourself to obvious sources—explore creatively and discover sources we haven't considered.

**Why This Matters**: BookBridge needs to become a must-have app. That happens when stories are so good, learners can't help but share them. Your discoveries will determine whether we have 50+ exceptional stories or 50+ mediocre ones.

---

## Context

**What We're Building**: BookBridge is an ESL learning app that needs 50-100 modern, emotionally impactful stories. We extract themes from sources and rewrite them as original narratives (no copyright issues).

**What Makes a Great Story**:
- ✅ **Story-driven** (first-person narratives, emotional journeys) NOT fact-driven (biographies, encyclopedias)
- ✅ **Modern relevance** (2020s preferred, 2010s acceptable) - stories that feel current
- ✅ **Clear emotional arc**: struggle → perseverance → breakthrough
- ✅ **5-7 specific emotional moments** (not vague, specific moments)
- ✅ **ESL resonance**: Themes like immigration, education, overcoming barriers, communication challenges
- ✅ **Length potential**: Can reach 15-45 minutes when fully developed
- ✅ **"Text a friend" factor**: Would someone share this story?

**Target Themes**: Immigration, education, transformation, family, health, community impact

---

## What "Exceptional" Looks Like: Helen Keller Benchmark

**Reference Example**: Helen Keller's "The Story of My Life" (Chapters III-IV) represents the gold standard:

**Why It's Exceptional**:
- ✅ **Story-driven**: First-person emotional journey (struggle → breakthrough), not fact-driven biography
- ✅ **Clear emotional arc**: Isolation/frustration → Perseverance → Breakthrough (discovery of language)
- ✅ **5-7 emotional moments**: Daily outbursts, parents' despair, meeting Dr. Bell, Anne Sullivan's arrival, "water" moment, realization, learning joy
- ✅ **ESL resonance**: 3+ multipliers (communication barriers, education journey, persistence)
- ✅ **"Text a friend" factor**: "You have to read this story about how Helen learned language"
- ✅ **Length**: 15-20 minutes (A1 level), perfect engagement

**Your Goal**: Find stories with this same emotional power and ESL relevance, but from modern sources (2020s preferred, 2010s acceptable).

## What Makes a Great Source

**Source Criteria** (not a list—use these to discover):
- **Story-driven content**: First-person narratives, emotional journeys, NOT fact-driven biographies
- **Modern relevance**: 2020s preferred, 2010s acceptable—stories that feel current
- **Accessible**: Can we actually get the content? (API, download, manual access)
- **Rich emotional moments**: Struggles, breakthroughs, transformations
- **ESL resonance potential**: Themes like immigration, education, overcoming barriers

**Think Beyond Obvious Sources**: 
- Don't limit yourself to journalism, documentaries, podcasts
- Explore: oral histories, community archives, user-generated content, social media stories, video platforms, educational content, nonprofit archives, government archives, interview collections
- **Question to guide you**: "Where do people share powerful personal stories?"

---

## Research Tasks

### Task 1: Creative Exploration (4 hours)

**Your Goal**: Discover ANY source type that might contain story-driven content. Be creative and exploratory.

**What to Do**:
- Explore sources we haven't considered—think outside the box
- Look for sources with emotional depth, not just information
- Document access methods for each source (API, manual download, web scraping, etc.)
- Focus on finding sources where people share powerful personal narratives
- **Key Question**: "Where do people share powerful personal stories?"

**What to Document**:
- Source name and type
- Access method (how do we get the content?)
- Why it might contain great stories
- Preliminary assessment: Does it seem story-driven?

### Task 2: Source Evaluation (3 hours)

**Your Goal**: Assess each discovered source for story quality and accessibility.

**What to Evaluate**:
- **Content quality**: Does it contain story-driven content (not facts)?
- **Length potential**: Can stories reach 15-45 minutes when fully developed?
- **Modern relevance**: Are stories from 2020s or 2010s?
- **Theme diversity**: What themes are represented? (immigration, education, transformation, etc.)
- **Accessibility**: How easy is it to access the content?

**What to Create**:
- Prioritization matrix: Rank sources by (ease of access × story quality × modern relevance)
- **Key Question**: "Would stories from this source pass the 'text a friend' test?"

### Task 3: Story Discovery (3 hours)

**Your Goal**: Identify 50+ specific stories from discovered sources.

**What to Do**:
- Find specific stories (not just sources)
- Document story titles, sources, and access methods
- Preliminary emotional impact assessment: Does it have a clear emotional arc?
- ESL resonance multiplier identification: Which multipliers apply?
- **Key Question**: "Is this story as emotionally powerful as Helen Keller's?"

**What to Document for Each Story**:
- Story title
- Source (where you found it)
- Access method (how to get it)
- Preliminary emotional arc assessment
- ESL resonance multipliers (which ones apply?)
- Length estimate (can it reach 15-45 minutes?)
