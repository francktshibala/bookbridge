## 🎯 Finding New Similar Voices: Search Strategy

### **Step 1: ElevenLabs Voice Library Search**

1. **Filter by Professional Usage:**
   - Sort by "Credits per User" (descending)
   - Look for voices with >3,000 credits/user
   - Check "Total Credits" >100M

2. **Filter by Characteristics:**
   - Search for: "audiobook", "narrator", "professional", "storytelling"
   - Look for: "calm", "warm", "clear", "sophisticated"
   - Avoid: "energetic", "dramatic", "intense" (unless needed)

3. **Check Voice Descriptions:**
   - Look for explicit mentions: "perfect for audiobooks", "ideal for narration"
   - Check age/tone descriptions: mature, professional, calm
   - Verify accent: neutral, clear, professional

### **Step 2: Test Voice Settings**

Test with Jane's proven settings:
```javascript
{
  stability: 0.5,
  similarity_boost: 0.8,
  style: 0.05,
  use_speaker_boost: true
}
```

**What to Listen For:**
- ✅ Natural cadence (not robotic)
- ✅ Clear pronunciation (ESL-friendly)
- ✅ Balanced warmth/authority
- ✅ Comfortable pacing
- ✅ Engaging without overwhelming

### **Step 3: Compare Against Jane**

Generate the same test text with:
1. Jane (baseline)
2. New voice candidate
3. Listen side-by-side

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Naturalness: Does it feel human?
- Clarity: Is pronunciation clear?
- Engagement: Is it listenable for 10+ minutes?
- ESL Suitability: Is it non-intimidating?
