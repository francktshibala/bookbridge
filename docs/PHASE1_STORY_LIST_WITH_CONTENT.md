**URL:** https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/7256

### Full Story Content:
*(Note: This is a summary - full text available at Project Gutenberg)*

Della and Jim are a young married couple living in a small apartment. They are very poor. Christmas is coming, and Della wants to buy Jim a perfect gift. She has only $1.87 saved. Her most prized possession is her beautiful long hair that reaches below her knees.

Della decides to sell her hair to a wig maker for $20. With this money, she buys Jim a platinum watch chain for his gold watch, which is his most prized possession.

When Jim comes home, he sees Della's short hair and is shocked. He gives Della her gift: beautiful combs for her long hair. But Della's hair is gone. Then Della gives Jim the watch chain. But Jim reveals he sold his watch to buy the combs.

Both have sacrificed their most prized possessions to buy gifts for each other. They realize their love is more valuable than any material thing.

## Story 2: "The Last Leaf" by O. Henry (1907)

**Source:** Project Gutenberg (in "The Trimmed Lamp" collection)  
**License:** ✅ Public domain  
**Length:** ~1,500 words (~12-15 minutes reading time)  
**URL:** Available in O. Henry collections on Project Gutenberg

### Full Story Content:
*(Note: Full text available at Project Gutenberg)*

In Greenwich Village, two young artists, Sue and Johnsy, share a studio apartment. Johnsy becomes very sick with pneumonia. The doctor says her chance of survival is only one in ten. Johnsy loses hope and believes she will die when the last leaf falls from the ivy vine outside her window.

Sue tries to give Johnsy hope, but Johnsy counts the leaves falling. "When the last leaf falls, I will die," she says.

Their neighbor, an old artist named Behrman, has always wanted to paint a masterpiece but never succeeded. When Sue tells him about Johnsy's belief, Behrman is angry. "Such nonsense!" he says.

That night, a terrible storm comes. The next morning, one last leaf remains on the vine. Johnsy sees it and begins to hope. The leaf stays for days, giving Johnsy strength. She recovers.

Later, Sue tells Johnsy the truth: Behrman painted that last leaf on the wall during the storm. He caught pneumonia and died, but he created his masterpiece - the leaf that saved Johnsy's life.

## Story 3: "After Twenty Years" by O. Henry (1906)

**Source:** Project Gutenberg (in "The Four Million" collection)  
**License:** ✅ Public domain  
**Length:** ~1,200 words (~10-15 minutes reading time)  
**URL:** Available in O. Henry collections on Project Gutenberg

### Full Story Content:
*(Note: Full text available at Project Gutenberg)*

A policeman walks his beat on a dark street. He sees a man waiting in a doorway. The man explains he's waiting for his friend Jimmy Wells. Twenty years ago, they made a promise to meet here exactly twenty years later.

The man tells the story: "Twenty years ago tonight, I dined here with Jimmy Wells, my best friend. We were both eighteen. I was leaving for the West to make my fortune. Jimmy said, 'We'll meet here again in exactly twenty years, no matter what happens.'"

The policeman listens, then continues his beat. The waiting man lights a cigar, revealing a diamond pin and expensive watch - signs of success.

A tall man approaches. "Is that you, Bob?" he asks. The waiting man thinks it's Jimmy, but something seems wrong. They walk together, talking about their lives.

When they reach a well-lit area, the waiting man realizes this isn't Jimmy. The man reveals he's a police officer. "You've been under arrest for ten minutes, Bob. But before we go, here's a note from Jimmy Wells."

The note reads: "Bob, I was at the place on time. When I saw your face in the light, I recognized you as the man wanted by Chicago police. I couldn't arrest you myself, so I got a plainclothes officer to do it. I was true to our appointment."

## Story 4: "The Story of an Hour" by Kate Chopin (1894)

**Source:** Project Gutenberg (public domain)

**Length:** ~1,000 words (~8-10 minutes, expandable to 15-20 min with context)  
**URL:** Available on Project Gutenberg

### Full Story Content:
*(Note: Full text available at Project Gutenberg)*

Mrs. Mallard has a heart condition. Her sister Josephine and her husband's friend Richards come to tell her that her husband Brently has been killed in a train accident. They break the news gently, afraid the shock will kill her.

Mrs. Mallard weeps at first, then goes to her room alone. She sits in a chair looking out the window. She sees spring outside - trees, birds, blue sky. She feels something strange coming to her.

At first, she doesn't know what it is. Then she realizes: she is free. Free from her husband. Free to live for herself. She thinks about the years ahead - years that belong only to her.

She whispers: "Free! Body and soul free!"

Her sister knocks on the door, worried. Mrs. Mallard opens the door, her eyes bright. She walks downstairs with her sister.

The front door opens. It's Brently Mallard - alive. He wasn't on the train. The news was wrong.

When Mrs. Mallard sees him, she dies. The doctors say she died of joy - the joy that kills.

## Story 5: "The Necklace" by Guy de Maupassant (1884)

**Source:** Project Gutenberg (public domain)

**Length:** ~3,000 words (~20-25 minutes reading time)  
**URL:** Available on Project Gutenberg

*(Note: Full text available at Project Gutenberg - already in app)*

Mathilde Loisel is a pretty woman married to a clerk. She dreams of wealth and luxury but lives in poverty. She's unhappy with her simple life.

Her husband gets an invitation to a fancy ball. Mathilde is upset because she has nothing to wear. Her husband gives her money for a dress. She borrows a diamond necklace from a wealthy friend, Madame Forestier.

At the ball, Mathilde is beautiful and happy. Everyone admires her. She dances all night.

When they return home, Mathilde realizes the necklace is gone. They search everywhere but can't find it.

They decide to replace it. They find a similar necklace for 36,000 francs - a fortune. They borrow money, buy the necklace, and return it to Madame Forestier, who doesn't notice the difference.

For ten years, they work to pay off the debt. Mathilde becomes old and tired. She does hard work, washing dishes and cleaning.

One day, Mathilde meets Madame Forestier, who still looks young and beautiful. Mathilde tells her the truth about the necklace. Madame Forestier reveals the original necklace was fake - worth only 500 francs.
